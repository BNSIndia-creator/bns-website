Safe & Inclusive Culture

Prevention of Sexual Harassment (PSH) Awareness Programs

Bharatiya Navchetana Sangh (BNS) conducts Prevention of Sexual Harassment (PSH) awareness programs to promote safe, respectful, and inclusive environments in educational institutions, workplaces, and communities.

These programs aim to educate individuals about respectful behavior, gender sensitivity, legal rights, and responsible conduct.

Through awareness, dialogue, and training, BNS helps organizations and institutions build cultures where dignity, equality, and safety are valued by everyone.

Why PSH Awareness is Important

Sexual harassment can have serious emotional, psychological, and professional consequences for individuals and communities.

  • Prevent inappropriate behavior and misconduct
  • Promote respect and gender equality
  • Encourage safe reporting of incidents
  • Strengthen institutional policies and responsibilities
  • Build safe and inclusive environments for all

PSH awareness programs help create communities where every individual feels safe, respected, and empowered.

PSH Programs for Educational Institutions

High Schools and Colleges

BNS conducts interactive sessions for students to promote respectful relationships, responsible behavior, and gender sensitivity.

Key topics include:

  • Understanding sexual harassment and inappropriate conduct
  • Respectful communication and personal boundaries
  • Responsible use of digital and social media platforms
  • Building safe campus environments
  • Awareness of legal protections and support systems

PSH Programs for Corporate Organizations

BNS also conducts Prevention of Sexual Harassment awareness programs for corporate employees and management teams.

These sessions help organizations strengthen workplace culture and comply with legal requirements.

Topics include:

  • Understanding workplace sexual harassment
  • Rights and responsibilities of employees and employers
  • Creating respectful workplace behavior
  • Role of Internal Complaints Committees (ICC)
  • Building inclusive and professional workplace environments

Program Approach

  • Awareness presentations
  • Interactive discussions
  • Real-life case examples
  • Policy guidance for institutions
  • Responsible workplace and campus culture building

Who Can Participate

  • High school students
  • College students and faculty
  • Corporate employees and managers
  • Educational institutions
  • Community organizations

Outcomes of the Program

  • Better understanding of respectful behavior
  • Awareness of legal rights and protections
  • Confidence to respond appropriately to misconduct
  • Ability to promote safe environments within institutions

Organize a PSH Awareness Program

Educational institutions and organizations can partner with BNS to conduct Prevention of Sexual Harassment awareness programs for students, faculty, and employees.

These sessions strengthen awareness, promote respectful conduct, and support safe and inclusive environments.
