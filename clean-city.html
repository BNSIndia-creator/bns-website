Clean City Community Partnership Program

Urban cleanliness is a shared responsibility between governments, communities, businesses, and civil society.

The BNS Clean City Initiative mobilizes communities to strengthen municipal sanitation systems through awareness, participation, and behavior change.

Upcoming Program Timeline: June – December 2026

Key Challenges

  • Mixed household waste
  • Plastic pollution
  • Waste dumping near drainage systems
  • Market garbage accumulation
  • Construction and demolition waste dumping

Many of these problems are behavioral rather than infrastructure-related. Addressing them requires collective participation and community awareness.

Community-Based Solution

The BNS model focuses on four key components:

Awareness

Educating communities about responsible waste management and sanitation practices.

Behavior Change

Encouraging citizens to adopt sustainable habits such as waste segregation.

Community Monitoring

Involving residents in monitoring cleanliness and reporting issues.

Impact Measurement

Tracking improvements in sanitation practices and community participation.

Partnership Model

Municipal Corporation

Waste collection coordination, sanitation infrastructure, and regulatory support.

BNS NGO

Community awareness campaigns, volunteer mobilization, and program coordination.

Citizens & Housing Societies

Waste segregation, responsible disposal, and participation in community cleanliness drives.

Businesses & Markets

Maintaining clean premises and responsible waste management practices.
